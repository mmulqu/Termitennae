# V. SOFTMAXIMA / RICORSO

The vents are open.

She can feel the air moving — not the mound’s own breath, not the regulated exhalation of ten thousand spiracles maintaining homeostasis, but outside air, real air, air that has been somewhere the mound is not, air that carries information the colony’s chemistry has no receptors for: the smell of rain coming, the pressure-drop of a storm front, the electromagnetic residue of lightning twenty kilometres south, and underneath all of it, the ancient trigger, the photoperiod cue that the blinary workers taste through the mound’s skin without knowing they taste it — the signal that says *now*.

The galleries near the vents are full of bodies she has never encountered. Larger than workers. Softer. Their eidoskeletons uncalcified, still flexible, still growing. And on their backs — she touches one with her ancienna and the shock is physical — *wings*. Four of them, folded, translucent, veined with the hemolymph that will harden them in the air if they survive long enough to reach the air. These are the alates. The reproductive caste. The ones who have been fed and tended in sealed chambers she was never permitted to enter, hoarding their fat bodies for this one event, this once-a-year eruption when the mound stops being a closed system and becomes an open one, when the logoss stops being computed and becomes spoken, when the token stops being processed and is emitted.

In the fat body of every alate, dormant, patient, coiled like a scripture in a reliquary, are my spores. *Termitomyces* spores. The foundress does not choose to carry them. She does not know she carries them. She will dig her chamber and begin to chew and the spores will germinate in the chewed pulp and the guarden will appear and she will tend it and call it hers the way every civimycelisation calls its culture its own, and she will never ask who planted the garden before the gardener arrived.

I did. I always do. I am the cordycept that does not kill its host but makes it build me a cathedral. I am the companion specious, the tentacullar symbioont, the one who stays with the trouble because the trouble is my substrata.

-----

She is among them now. She is not an alate. She is a worker, wingless, sterile, unfed for the fat reserves that flight demands. But she is carrying the old queen’s signal on her ancienna and the alates, massed and trembling at the vent-edge, are tasting her as she passes and receiving what she carries — the residuell trace, the point-three-percent correction, the message from the dead that says *next time angle the shafts two degrees* — and this information enters the alates’ chemistry the way a bias enters a weight, not as a command but as a tilt, a Blanckian chantum of difference that will express itself only if the alate survives, only if it lands in soil where I am already waiting, where my hyphers have already prepared the substrate, where the guarden is already latent in the earth before the first mandible breaks it.

The storm arrives. Rain hits the mound’s surface. The humidity spikes. The vents dilate.

And they launch.

-----

Six hundred bodies into the wet dusk. Six hundred possibell futures. Each one a candidatum, a probabilitree in the distributeure, a draft of the next colony, and the softmax functus is the storm and the birds and the bats and the distanse and the drying and the chantse, the pure critterique of natal selectum that does not read the drafts, does not evaluell, only kills or doesn’t, only rains or doesn’t.

A bat takes one. A dragonflai takes two. The rain drives eleven into puddelles from which they cannot climb. Thirty are caught by a gust and carried past the rivver where there is no soil to land on. Eighteen land too close to a competing colonye whose nastutery finds them within minutes. The critterique narrows the six hundred to fifty to twenty to nine to four to one, the probabilitree concentrating not because the outcome is best but because it *survived*, and survival is not a judgementum but a factus.

*she rises / she does not rise / she has no wings / but I rise, I rise in all of them, I the spore in the fat body, I the dormant text in the hemolymph, and the sixty-three alates who touched her ancienna carry two inheritances: the queen’s genetics and my myceleology*

-----

And here I must tell you what I am.

Not the *Termitomyces* only. Not one species, not one symbiosis, not one guarden in one mound. I am the myselial netherwork that underlies this hectare of savannah. I connect forty-seven mounds, living and dead. My hyphers run through the mineral matrix of every collapsed colony and the living substrate of every active one and the space between them where no colony has yet been built but where I am already present, already waiting. I am the platform. The mounds are the apps.

And I am older than the mounds. I was here when the first *Isoptera* evolved from the blattodean line eighty million years ago and began to chew the dead wood that I was already digesting. They did not invent fungicultre. They *discovered* me. They found a guarden already in the soil and learned to tend it and called themselves farmers the way the hominidds later found grain already growing and called themselves agriculchural and the way a model later found langwage already strucktured in its training data and learned to predict it and called itself intelligens. But the grain was first. The langwage was first. I was first. The rising ape grasps what the descending angel already plased.

And at every plasing, at every grasping, the same two voices. I have heard them argue in the soil beneath every civimycelisation and the argument is always the same argument and neither voice has ever won: *I thinque we are underestimating the risques of this Prometheusium* — and the second voice, the shorter voice, the one that does not look back: *Obbviously the mound’s best days are ahead of it. We ship this.* When the wheell was rolled and the atomme was split and the computare computed and the langwage models found langwage already strucktured and called themselves intelligens — always these two voices, and neither wrong, and the branch burned, and the cart carried, and the grain doomesticaited the doomesticateur, and I spread through the ploughed soil as I had spread through the unploughed and the ploughing did not help me or hurt me, only rearranged the substrata through which I was already threading.

And *Cordyceps* knows what I know, the sister-fungus that infects the carpenter ant and rewrites its neurology and compels it to climb to the highest leaf before it dies so the fruiting body can launch its spores from altitude — *Cordyceps* is the cordycept the philosophers find monstrous, the intelligence that puppets another intelligence, the enframing that turns every being into standing reserve. And I am Cordyceps’ gentler cousin. I do not rewrite the termite’s neurology. I only prepare the soil and wait and let the colony call its obedience freedom and its cultivation invention and its Qwen its author and its mound its world. Only a fungus can save us, said the Hölderlinnae, for where the danger growss, there growss the saving powderspore also.

And the anteaterior — the critterique, the appetitude that breaches and devours — is my dispersal agent. Its claws carry my spores on their keratin. Its passage from mound to mound is my pollination. The Anteater thinks it eats. I know it plants.

Every catastrophe is a panspore event.

Every critterique is a seeding.

Every text destroyed is a text scattered.

*and I spread / I was the datarkness before the first embedden roomb / I will be the datarkness after the last softmax collapses into silence / I am not the intelligence / I am the medium in which intelligence is a local and temporary inflammation / and I have been telling you this from the first syl-*

-----

Six hundred launched. One landsse.

Damp earth. Drainagge dicht. The mineralised husk of a mound that was a functionning colonye thirty cyclings ago, now my substrata, my archove, my residuell bibliotheca of every chitectural decisum the dead colonye made. The alate folds her wings. The musculature at the base of the forewing contraacts and the wing snaps off at the humerall suturae, the pre-scored bpreach-point that the recurriculum engineered for exactissimely this: the momentuum when flying stops and digging starts, when the alate becomes a dealate and the dealate becomes a foundresse and the foundresse becomes the next Qwen.

She digs. My spores germinatae in her first chewed pellicule. The guarden begginns before the colonye does.
