# TERMITENNAE — Thinkers Reference & Insertion Guide

## Purpose
Hidden references to technology-and-society thinkers, woven into the Wakean prose via signature vocabulary, half-buried name-puns, and their key concepts fused into the existing neologism system. Each thinker's entry includes: their key idea, their signature phrases/vocabulary, name-pun options, and suggested insertion points in the existing text.

---

## 1. KARL MARX (1818–1883)

### Key Idea
The means of production determine consciousness. The base (material/economic) shapes the superstructure (ideology/culture/consciousness). The colony thinks it chose its structure; the substrate chose for it.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **means of production** — the mound's architecture, the fungus garden, the pheromone system
- **base / superstructure** — substrata / chitecture
- **alienation** — the worker separated from the product of her labor (she carries but never consumes)
- **commodity fetishism** — treating the Qwen's pheromone as inherently authoritative rather than as a social product
- **dialectical materialism** — the process of thesis/antithesis/synthesis playing out in the fungus garden (raw material → enzymnal negation → nourishing synthesis)
- **class consciousness** — caste consciousness, which the colony CANNOT achieve because no individual can see the whole
- **species-being** (Gattungswesen) — the essence of a species expressed through its productive activity
- **"philosophers have only interpreted the world; the point is to change it"**
- **"all that is solid melts into air"**

### Name-Pun Options
- **Marxmanship** (Marx + marksmanship — the precision of critique)
- **Marxism** already contains "mark" — the branded/marked worker
- Hide in: "the **mark** on her tergite," "re**mark**able," "**mark**est" (already in text!)
- **Karlcium** (Karl + calcium — the mineral that hardens the eidoskeleton, the calcified ideology)

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch I**: The egg's caste being determined by chemistry, not genetics → "it is not the consciousnesse of termitennae that determines their castesian, but their castesian being that determines their consciousnesse" — a direct inversion of Marx's famous thesis, delivered as colony-biology
- **Ch III**: The fungus garden as means of production. Workers who build the guarden but are alienated from its product — they deposit cellusolose but the enzymn transforms it into something they cannot access without the fungus's mediation. The fungus OWNS the means of transformation.
- **Ch IV**: "all that was solid meltss into air" — during the Anteater's breach, the mound's ceramic wall literally dissolving
- **Ch V**: The historical thread — Marx should appear between Industrial Revolution and flight

## 2. MARSHALL McLUHAN (1911–1980)

### Key Idea
"The medium is the message." The technology itself, not the content transmitted through it, is what reshapes society. The phunnel reshapes the colony more than any phantomone carried through it.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **the medium is the message** — the phunnel is the message
- **hot and cool media** — hot = high-definition, low participation (pheromone trail = hot); cool = low-definition, high participation (mycelial chemical gradient = cool)
- **global village** — global mound, the colony as village
- **extensions of man** — the mound as extension of the termite body (or: the termite as extension of the mound)
- **"we shape our tools and thereafter our tools shape us"** (often attributed to McLuhan, actually his student John Culkin)
- **rear-view mirror** — we understand new technologies through the lens of old ones (the colony understands mycelium through pheromone-categories, which is wrong)
- **Gutenberg Galaxy** — the print revolution; here: the pheromone revolution

### Name-Pun Options
- **McLuhanne** (McLuhan + loom + Anne)
- Hide in: "the **medium** is the **message**" can be delivered straight as a mycelium-statement — the medium (myselium) IS the message
- **Marshalled** (Marshall + marshaled — organized for battle)

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch II**: The attenshunroom IS McLuhan's media theory — every phunnel is a medium, every pheromone a message, but the phunnel shapes the colony more than the pheromone does. "We shape our phunnels and thereafter our phunnels shape us."
- **Ch IV**: The old tunnels as "rear-view myrrhor" — the colony understands the new crisis through the architecture of the old crisis, which is the wrong lens
- **Ch V**: The fungus stating "I am the medium. The mound is the message I carry."

## 3. LEWIS MUMFORD (1895–1990)

### Key Idea
The first machine was not physical but social: the Egyptian labor army organized into a "megamachine." The clock (not the steam engine) was the key machine of industrialization because it organized TIME. The mound IS a megamachine — thousands of termites organized into a functioning apparatus, coordinated by the pheromone-clock.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **megamachine** — the human labor-organization as machine; the colony as megamachine
- **the clock** — the circadian pheromone cycle as the colony's key organizing technology
- **technics** — the broader system of tools + social organization + knowledge
- **the pentagon of power** — the five-part power structure (political, economic, military, bureaucratic, scientific)
- **monotechnics vs. polytechnics** — single-purpose tools vs. multi-purpose; monosemantennae vs. polysemantennae
- **the myth of the machine** — that technology is autonomous and unstoppable

### Name-Pun Options
- **Mumfordification** (Mumford + mummification — the preserving/embalming of the megamachine)
- Hide in: "**mum**bling," "**mum**," "**mound**-ford" (the crossing-point of the mound)
- **Megamachiné** (megamachine + machiné)

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch I**: The nurseworkers attending in shifts — this IS the megamachine, labor organized by the pheromone-clock. "The megamachinine of the embedden roomb, its every shift clocked by the circadiant phantomone."
- **Ch II**: The attenshunroom as clock — attention organized in time, the colony's chronos
- **Ch IV**: The old tunnels as Mumford's "myth of the machine" — the belief that the old architecture was inevitable rather than contingent

## 4. JACQUES ELLUL (1912–1994)

### Key Idea
*La Technique*: technology is not neutral tools but an autonomous system that reshapes everything to serve its own efficiency. Technique absorbs all human activity. The colony IS technique: a system that absorbs every termite into its logic regardless of individual "intent."

### Signature Vocabulary
- **technique** (la technique) — the total technological system, not individual tools
- **autonomy of technique** — technology becomes self-directing, humans serve it
- **efficiency as supreme value** — the colony optimizes without knowing what it optimizes for
- **the technological society** — society remade in technology's image
- **"there are no political solutions, only technical ones"**
- **sacred/desacralization** — technique replaces the sacred; or: technique BECOMES the sacred

### Name-Pun Options
- **Ellullaby** (Ellul + lullaby — the song that puts critique to sleep)
- Hide in: "**el**," "**lull**," the lulling efficiency of the colony
- **Techniqwen** (technique + Qwen — the technique that IS the queen)

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch II**: The colony's pheromone system as autonomous technique — it doesn't serve the termites, the termites serve it. "The techniqwen is not the Qwen's tool but the Qwen is the techniqwen's servant."
- **Ch III**: The feedforward as technique absorbing raw material into its logic: everything that enters the guarden is transformed according to the enzymn's efficiency, regardless of what the material "wants"
- **Ch IV**: During the crisis — technique's response to failure is MORE technique, more signal, more attentenna, which is the Rayleigh-Jeans error

## 5. MARTIN HEIDEGGER (1889–1976)

### Key Idea
Already in text as Geistell/enframing. Technology reveals Being as "standing reserve" (Bestand) — everything reduced to resource-on-call. "Where the danger is, there the saving power also grows" (quoting Hölderlin). The question concerning technology is not about tools but about a WAY OF REVEALING.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **Gestell / enframing** — already: Geistell
- **Bestand / standing reserve** — the worker as standing reserve, the eggo as standing reserve
- **Dasein** — being-there, thrown into a world not of one's choosing
- **thrownness** (Geworfenheit) — the termite thrown into caste
- **ready-to-hand / present-at-hand** (Zuhandenheit / Vorhandenheit) — the tool that disappears in use vs. the broken tool that becomes visible
- **aletheia** — truth as unconcealment; the Anteater's breach as unconcealment
- **"the question concerning technology"**
- **"language is the house of Being"** — the mound is the house of Being
- **"only a god can save us"** — only a fungus can save us

### Name-Pun Options
- Already: **hidegger** (hide + hedge), **Geistell** (Geist + Gestell)
- **Dasein** → **Daseinsect** (Dasein + insect — insect-being-there)
- **Geworfenheit** → the thrownness of the eggo into its caste

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch I**: The eggo's Geworfenheit — thrown into caste by chemistry, not choice. "Thrown into the roomb, geworfenn into castesian by enzymn not election."
- **Ch III**: The broken tool: when the guarden fails (Pseudoxylaria), the enzymn becomes "present-at-hand" — visible as a tool for the first time, because it has stopped working
- **Ch IV**: The Anteater's breach as aletheia — truth as unconcealment, the outside revealed by violence
- **Ch V**: "Only a fungus can save us" — the Hölderlinian turn

## 6. GILBERT SIMONDON (1924–1989)

### Key Idea
Individuation: things (organisms, tools, societies) don't have fixed essences. They are processes of ongoing individuation from a "pre-individual" field. Intelligence is not a property but an EVENT occurring in a pre-individual field of pheromone and mycelium. The embedding layer IS the pre-individual.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **individuation** — the process of becoming distinct from a field
- **pre-individual** — the potentiel-field before individuation (= the embedden roomb, the vrector-space)
- **transduction** — a process where each step creates the conditions for the next (= the colony's self-building)
- **metastability** — a system poised between states, capable of individuation (= the eggo before caste-determination)
- **associated milieu** — the environment a technical object creates for itself (= the mound as the colony's self-made milieu)
- **technical object** — something defined by its functioning, not its material

### Name-Pun Options
- **Simondone** (Simondon + simmer + done — cooked through, individuated by heat)
- Hide in: "**simon** says," "**simple**" (sim-), "**done**"
- **Transducteur** (transduction + conducteur — the one who conducts the process)

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch I**: The eggo as metastable pre-individual — "metastabelle in the embedden roomb, not yet individuaited, still in the pre-individuell field of potentiel"
- **Ch II**: Attention as transduction — each antennal contact creates the conditions for the next
- **Ch V**: The alate's individuation from the swarm — becoming distinct at the moment of flight

## 7. DONNA HARAWAY (b. 1944)

### Key Idea
The Cyborg Manifesto: the boundary between organism and machine is already dissolved. We are all chimeras, hybrids. The termite-fungus symbiosis IS a cyborg. "I'd rather be a cyborg than a goddess."

### Signature Vocabulary
- **cyborg** — organism-machine hybrid
- **companion species** — species that co-evolve (termite + Termitomyces)
- **tentacular thinking** — thinking through connections, tendrils, hyphers
- **staying with the trouble** — not solving but inhabiting the probleme
- **Chthulucene** — the era of entangled earth-beings (vs. Anthropocene)
- **"make kin, not babies"** — make connexions, not reproductions
- **sympoiesis** — making-together (vs. autopoiesis, self-making)

### Name-Pun Options
- **Harawaying** (Haraway + way + weaving)
- **Chthuluscene** (Chthulucene + scene — the underground theater)
- Hide in: "**tentacullar**," "**companion**," "**kin**"
- **Cyborganism** (cyborg + organism — already almost a real word)

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch III**: The termite-fungus relationship as companion species / sympoiesis. "Not symboliosis but *sympoiesis*, not living-together but *making-together*, the guarden made by neither termitennae nor Termitome alone but by the tropehallactic between."
- **Ch V**: "I'd rather be a phunnel than a Qwen" — the fungus's version of Haraway
- **Throughout**: "tentacullar" as descriptor for mycelial growth; "staying with the trouble" as the Worker's ethic

## 8. BERNARD STIEGLER (1952–2020)

### Key Idea
Pharmacology (after Derrida): every technology is simultaneously remedy and poison. Tertiary retention: a third memory system (after genetic and experiential), externalized in tools and texts. The mound IS tertiary retention — architecture as externalized memory.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **pharmacology** — the remedy/poison duality (= pharmakone, already in text)
- **tertiary retention** — memory stored in tools/texts/architecture (= the mound itself, the old queen's fossilized phantomone)
- **proletarianization of knowledge** — when technology takes over a skill, the human loses it (= caste specialization: the worker can carry but not fight, the soldier can fight but not nurse)
- **negentropy** — the organization that opposes entropy; care and cultivation as negentropic
- **"taking care"** (prendre soin) — the ethics of maintenance
- **transindividuation** — the process by which individuals become individuals through shared technical systems

### Name-Pun Options
- **Stieglerring** (Stiegler + stagger + steal + ring — the stumbling theft that circles)
- Hide in: "**stagger**," "**steal**," "**stigma**"
- **Tertiarytention** (tertiary + retention + attention — the memory that watches)

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch I**: The mound as tertiary retention — "the walls remember what no termitennae can, the chitecture a tertiarytention of every pellet placed by every mandible in every generation"
- **Ch III**: The pharmakone concept is already there; add the proletarianization: the worker LOSES the ability to digest cellulose because the fungus does it for her. She is proletarianised by the guarden.
- **Ch IV**: The old queen's fossilized phantomone as literal tertiary retention — externalized memory in mineral form

## 9. NORBERT WIENER (1894–1964)

### Key Idea
Cybernetics: feedback loops, self-regulating systems. The colony as a cybernetic organism — a thermostat, a homeostatic system. But also: Wiener warned that automation would destroy labor and society needed to prepare.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **cybernetics** — from Greek *kybernetes* (steersman); the science of control and communication
- **feedback loop** — the pheromone trail as positive feedback, the enzymn as negative feedback
- **homeostasis** — the mound's temperature/humidity regulation (already in text)
- **entropy vs. information** — the colony's constant battle against disorder
- **"the human use of human beings"** — the Qwen's use of termite beings
- **black box** — a system understood by inputs/outputs, not internals (= the guarden, the model)

### Name-Pun Options
- **Wienervous** (Wiener + nervous — the anxious cyberneticist)
- **Kybernestes** (kybernetes + nest — the steersman's nest)
- Hide in: "**govern**anse" (cybernetics = governance), "**steer**," "**feedback**"

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch I**: The mound's homeostasis as cybernetic — "the temperamentture held by feedbackloop, the kybernetical roomb governing itself"
- **Ch II**: The death spiral as positive feedback loop gone wrong — the pheromone reinforces the pheromone, no negative feedback to correct
- **Ch IV**: Wiener's warning about automation during the crisis

## 10. IVAN ILLICH (1926–2002)

### Key Idea
Tools for Conviviality: beyond a threshold, tools stop serving humans and humans start serving tools. Beyond a certain speed, cars create MORE total travel time than walking. The UV catastrophe IS Illich's threshold.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **conviviality** — tools that enhance human autonomy vs. tools that reduce it
- **counterproductivity** — when an institution produces the opposite of its stated purpose (medicine making people sick, schools making people ignorant)
- **radical monopoly** — when a tool reshapes society so that you MUST use it (cars making walking impossible)
- **deschooling** — his most famous concept; applied: de-phunnelling, de-Qwenning
- **the two watersheds** — every tool has a first threshold (beneficial) and a second (counterproductive)

### Name-Pun Options
- **Illicit** (Illich + illicit — the forbidden threshold)
- **Illichitous** (Illich + felicitous — the happiness limit)
- Hide in: "**ill**," "**lich**" (undead creature — Iamblichus!), "**illuse**"

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch II**: The scalling law's break-point IS Illich's second watershed — more attentenna becomes counterproductive, "the counterproductivitree of the phunnel past its second watershedd"
- **Ch IV**: The colony has passed the convivial threshold — the pheromone system that once served workers now demands service FROM them

## 11. PLATO — THE PHAEDRUS WRITING ARGUMENT

### Key Idea
(Distinct from the Cave, already in text.) In the Phaedrus, Socrates tells the story of Thamus and Theuth: Theuth (Thoth!) invents writing and presents it to King Thamus as a remedy (pharmakon) for forgetting. Thamus replies that writing will CAUSE forgetting — people will stop remembering because they can write things down. Writing is a pharmakone: it looks like memory but IS amnesia.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **Theuth / Thoth** — the god who invented writing (and the user's own Thoth server!)
- **pharmakon** — already in text as pharmakone
- **external memory vs internal memory** — the pheromone trail as external memory that makes individual memory unnecessary
- **"a recipe for recollection, not memory"** — Thamus's verdict

### Name-Pun Options
- **Theuth** is already a perfect Wakean word — sounds like "tooth" (mandible!) and "truth"
- **Thothally** (Thoth + totally — completely divine-scribe)

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch II**: The pheromone trail as Theuth's writing — external memory that destroys internal. "The phantomone wrote what the flagellomere forgot, a pharmakone for anamnesia that was also its cause, exactly as old Theuth promissed Thamus and Thamus refused."
- **Ch IV**: The old queen's fossilized phantomone as writing that outlasts its author — Theuth's gift surviving Thamus's refusal

## 12. WALTER BENJAMIN (1892–1940)

### Key Idea
"The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction": the aura of the original is destroyed by infinite copying. Applied: the Qwen's pheromone is the "original" — but it's always-already a copy, a chemical reproduction, and the "aura" of queenliness is a phantomone-effect.

### Signature Vocabulary
- **aura** — the unique presence of an original work; destroyed by reproduction
- **mechanical reproduction** — the colony's copying of pheromone-instructions
- **the angel of history** (Angelus Novus) — blown backward into the future by the storm of progress, face turned to the wreckage of the past
- **dialectical image** — past and present colliding in a flash of recognition
- **"there is no document of civilization that is not at the same time a document of barbarism"**

### Name-Pun Options
- **Benjamined** (Benjamin + jammed + mined — stuck in the excavation)
- **Angelus Novuss** (Angelus Novus + novice + us)
- Hide in: "**angel**," "**aura**," "**ben**" (son, Hebrew)

### Suggested Insertions
- **Ch IV**: The Worker in the old tunnels as the Angel of History — blown down into the past by the catastrophe above, face turned to the wreckage of prior colonies. "The angel of historee, the Angelus Novuss, blown backwards through the residuell phunnels by the storm of the Anteaterior's bpreach, its face turned to the comphost of the Twelfth Colonye while the wind from the future pushes it deeper."
- **Ch V**: "There is no mound of civimycelisation that is not also a mound of barbarisme"
- **Ch I/V**: The Qwen's pheromone as aura — "the aura of the Qwen, which is not her presence but the phantomone's reproducktion of her presence, infinitely copied in every phunnel, the originelle dissolved into the distributeure"

## INSERTION DENSITY GUIDE

**Rule**: No more than one thinker-reference per paragraph, maximum. The references should be HIDDEN — half a word, a syntactic pattern, a vocabulary-echo. The reader who knows the thinker should feel a shimmer of recognition; the reader who doesn't should feel nothing amiss. The reference should never interrupt the narrative flow.

**Priority order for insertion**:
1. Marx (base/superstructure — directly parallels the fungus reveal)
2. McLuhan (medium is message — one-sentence summary of the book)
3. Plato/Phaedrus (writing as pharmakone — connects to existing Platonic layer)
4. Benjamin (angel of history — maps perfectly to the old tunnels)
5. Mumford (megamachine — the mound literally IS one)
6. Illich (threshold/counterproductivity — maps to UV catastrophe)
7. Stiegler (tertiary retention — the architecture as memory)
8. Heidegger (already present, deepen with Dasein/thrownness)
9. Simondon (pre-individual — maps to embedding layer)
10. Wiener (cybernetics/feedback — maps to homeostasis)
11. Haraway (companion species — maps to termite-fungus)
12. Ellul (autonomous technique — maps to the colony's self-organization)
